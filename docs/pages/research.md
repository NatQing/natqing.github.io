<header class="research-header">
  <h1>My research</h1>
  <p>My work sits at the interface of computational design and experimental bioengineering. I’m especially interested in biological systems that can be designed in silico, built in the lab, and evaluated quantitatively.</p>
</header>

<div class="research-list">
  <section class="research-entry">
    <h2>De Novo Design of Electron and Energy Conductive Protein Nanowires</h2>
    <p class="research-meta">UW Institute for Protein Design · November 2024–present</p>
    <p>I design and test electron- and energy-conductive protein nanowires under the guidance of Avi Swartz. My work includes an in-silico parametric design pipeline as well as wet-lab expression, purification, validation, and functional assays.</p>
  </section>
  <section class="research-entry">
    <h2>Phosphorylation-inducible heterodimers</h2>
    <p class="research-meta">IPD JUPITER Cohort · September 2024–June 2025</p>
    <p>Working with Kathryn Shelley and Cullen Demakis, I designed and tested phosphorylation-responsive heterodimer systems through the Institute for Protein Design’s undergraduate research cohort.</p>
  </section>
  <section class="research-entry">
    <h2>Biofabrication for tissue regeneration</h2>
    <p class="research-meta">OHSU Nakayama Lab · Summer 2021</p>
    <p>I engineered an open-source Voron 3D printer into a bioprinter and developed methods for autonomous fabrication of shear-induced scaffolding materials for regenerative medicine.</p>
  </section>
  <section class="research-entry">
    <h2>Synthetic biology with UW iGEM</h2>
    <p class="research-meta">University of Washington · September 2023–present</p>
    <p>I contributed to the research and wet-lab teams behind the gold-medal Might Moieties project, then worked on designing and testing de novo competitive inhibitors for ExoA secretion proteins.</p>
  </section>
  <section class="research-entry">
    <h2>Pulmonary Artery topology modeling</h2>
    <p class="research-meta">UW Medicine Zheng Lab · June 2024–September 2024</p>
    <p>With Dr. Sam Reyner, I modeled microscopic functional intravascular topologies to help characterize vascular structure and behavior.</p>
  </section>
</div>
