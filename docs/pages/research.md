<header class="research-header">
  <h1>My research</h1>
  <p>My work sits at the interface of computational design and experimental bioengineering. I’m especially interested in biological systems that can be designed in silico, built in the lab, and evaluated quantitatively.</p>
</header>

<div class="research-list">
  <section class="research-entry">
    <h2>De Novo Design of Electron and Energy Conductive Protein Nanowires</h2>
    <p class="research-meta">UW Baker Lab · November 2024–present</p>
    <p>I design and test electron- and energy-conductive protein nanowires under the guidance of Avi Swartz. I developed a computational pipeline combining ProteinMPNN, RFdiffusion, AlphaFold 3, and linear-algebra-based parametric design, then experimentally validate designs through cloning, protein expression and purification, fluorescence microscopy, and circular dichroism.</p>
  </section>
  <section class="research-entry">
    <h2>Phosphorylation-inducible heterodimers</h2>
    <p class="research-meta">IPD JUPITER Cohort · September 2024–June 2025</p>
    <p>Working with Kathryn Shelley and Cullen Demakis, I designed phosphorylation-responsive heterodimer systems using Crispy-Shifty, validated designs with ELISA assays, and presented the results at the Undergraduate Research Symposium.</p>
  </section>
  <section class="research-entry">
    <h2>Biofabrication for tissue regeneration</h2>
    <p class="research-meta">OHSU Nakayama Lab · June 2024–September 2024</p>
    <p>Working with Dr. Karina Nakayama, I engineered an open-source Voron 3D printer into a bioprinter and developed methods for autonomous fabrication of shear-induced scaffolding materials for tissue regeneration.</p>
  </section>
  <section class="research-entry">
    <h2>Synthetic biology with UW iGEM</h2>
    <p class="research-meta">University of Washington · September 2023–present</p>
    <p>I helped experimentally validate protein linkers for the gold-medal Mighty Moieties project, then led computational design of protein minibinders that competitively inhibit Pseudomonas aeruginosa Exotoxin A for the silver-medal ExAway project.</p>
  </section>
  <section class="research-entry">
    <h2>Intravascular topology modeling</h2>
    <p class="research-meta">UW Medicine Zheng Lab · September 2023–December 2024</p>
    <p>With Dr. Sam Reyner, I modeled microscopic functional intravascular topologies and received training in two-photon microscopy and organ-on-chip manufacturing.</p>
  </section>
</div>
